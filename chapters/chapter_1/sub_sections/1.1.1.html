Approach Comparison

Concept Top-Down Bottom-Up
Process Splitting (Decomposition) Merging (Integration)
Redundancy Contains redundant information Redundancy eliminated
Programming Languages Procedural (C) Object-Oriented (Python, Java)
Starting Point From overall problem From individual components
Testing Focus Unit testing first Integration testing first

The Top-Down approach begins with defining the overall structure and then breaking it down, while the Bottom-Up approach starts with individual components and builds them into a complete system.

Top-Down is ideal for problems with a clear overall structure, while Bottom-Up works well for problems where the basic components are well-understood but the overall structure might evolve.

Modularisation combines aspects of both approaches by organizing code into independent, reusable components that can be developed separately and then integrated into the full system.
